# Chapter 1: The Human Crisis
## Page 22
- For Camus, this meant working for “a worldwide abolition of capital punishment.”26 While Camus never expected to live in a world where there would be no killing, he argued that killing can never, must never, be justified. As he saw it, all killing is murder, and he found legal, efficient, institutionalized murder the most abhorrent.
- Camus never saw himself as a philosopher. He was certainly no academic. He came from poverty, from “ordinary” people, and it was to them he was most concerned to speak.
